### 22. Chain Reorganization (Reorg)

Chain reorganization (commonly called reorg) is a temporary change in the blockchain’s history when a node replaces its current chain with a longer or more valid chain.

---

## 1. What is Chain Reorganization?

A chain reorg happens when:

 Two miners produce blocks at nearly the same time
 The network briefly splits into two competing chains (forks)
 Later, one chain becomes longer → the network switches to it

👉 The shorter chain is discarded (or “orphaned”), and its transactions may return to the mempool

---

## 2. Simple Example (Step-by-Step)

Imagine this situation:

```
Block A → Block B → Block C
                  ↘ Block C'
```

### Step-by-step:

1. Two miners create:

    Block C
    Block C′ (at the same time)

2. Now the network is split:

    Some nodes follow C
    Others follow C′

3. A new block is mined on one branch:

```
Block A → B → C → D   (longer chain)
Block A → B → C′      (shorter chain)
```

4. Network rule:
   👉 Longest chain wins

5. Result:

 Chain with C′ is abandoned
 Its transactions go back to mempool (if not included elsewhere)

---

## 3. Why Reorg Happens

 Network latency (nodes receive blocks at different times)
 Simultaneous mining
 Natural behavior in decentralized systems
 Occasional mining race conditions

---

## 4. Types of Reorgs

### (a) Short Reorg (Common)

 1–2 blocks replaced
 Happens regularly in networks like Bitcoin
 Usually harmless

### (b) Deep Reorg (Rare & Dangerous)

 Many blocks replaced
 Can indicate:

   51% attack
   Network instability

---

## 5. Impact of Chain Reorganization

### 1. Transaction Reversal

 Transactions in orphaned blocks may disappear temporarily
 This is why confirmations matter

### 2. Double Spending Risk

 Attackers may exploit reorgs to reverse payments

### 3. Mempool Changes

 Removed transactions go back to mempool

---

## 6. Confirmations (Very Important Concept)

👉 A confirmation = number of blocks added after your transaction

Example:

 Your transaction is in block C
 After 5 more blocks → 5 confirmations

### Why it matters:

 More confirmations = lower chance of reorg affecting it

#### Common standards:

 Bitcoin: 6 confirmations
 Ethereum: ~12–15 confirmations

---

## 7. Relation to Forks

Reorg is closely related to forks:

| Concept | Meaning                             |
| ------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Fork    | Temporary split in chain            |
| Reorg   | Resolution of fork (one chain wins) |

👉 So:

 Fork happens first
 Reorg is the correction process
