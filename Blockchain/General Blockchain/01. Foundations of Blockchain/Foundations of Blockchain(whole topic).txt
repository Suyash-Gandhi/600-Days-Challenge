          Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT) Basics

## 1. Definition

Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT) is a digital system for recording and sharing data across multiple computers (nodes) where:

 Every participant has a copy of the ledger.
 All copies are synchronized.
 Changes require agreement (consensus) of the network.
 No single central authority controls it.

ğŸ‘‰ In short: DLT is like a shared notebook that everyone in a group keeps and updates together, instead of relying on a single central copy.

---

## 2. Key Features of DLT

1. Decentralization

    No central server or authority manages the ledger.
    All participants hold equal power in maintaining the system.

2. Transparency

    Every transaction is visible to authorized participants.
    Reduces the need for intermediaries.

3. Immutability

    Once recorded, transactions cannot be easily altered or deleted.
    Ensures trust and security.

4. Consensus Mechanism

    Rules/protocols (like Proof of Work, Proof of Stake, etc.) are used to agree on the validity of transactions.

5. Security via Cryptography

    Digital signatures and hashing ensure authenticity and prevent fraud.

---

## 3. How DLT Works (Step by Step)

1. A transaction/request is created (e.g., transfer of funds, update of supply chain data).
2. The transaction is broadcast to all participants (nodes) in the network.
3. The nodes verify the transaction using consensus rules.
4. Once validated, the transaction is recorded in the ledger of every node.
5. All nodes update their copies simultaneously â†’ creating a distributed, synchronized record.

---

## 4. Types of DLT

1. Blockchain (Most Common)

    Transactions are stored in â€œblocksâ€ linked together in a chain.
    Example: Bitcoin, Ethereum.

2. Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG)

    Transactions are stored as nodes in a graph instead of blocks.
    Faster and scalable compared to blockchain.
    Example: IOTA, Hedera Hashgraph.

3. Others (less common)

    Holochain, Tempo, etc. (experimental structures).

---

## 5. DLT vs Traditional Centralized Ledger

| Aspect       | Centralized Ledger (Bank/Company)  | Distributed Ledger (DLT)                       |
| ------------ | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Control      | Managed by one authority           | Shared among all participants                  |
| Trust        | Users must trust central authority | Trustless (relies on cryptography + consensus) |
| Transparency | Limited visibility                 | High transparency                              |
| Security     | Single point of failure            | Highly resilient to attacks                    |
| Cost         | Requires intermediaries            | Reduces intermediaries                         |
