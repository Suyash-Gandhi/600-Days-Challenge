## 28. Finality in Blockchain

Finality is one of the most important concepts in blockchain because it answers a critical question:

👉 “When can we be sure that a transaction is permanent and cannot be reversed?”

---

# 1. What is Finality?

Finality refers to the point at which a transaction becomes irreversible and permanently recorded on the blockchain.

 Before finality → transaction can still be changed or removed
 After finality → transaction is locked forever

### Simple Analogy

Think of writing with:

 Pencil → can erase (no finality)
 Pen → permanent (finality)

---

# 2. Why Finality is Important

Finality is crucial for trust in blockchain systems:

### 1. Prevents Double Spending

 Without finality, someone could reuse the same funds
 Finality ensures only one valid version of history exists

### 2. Builds Trust

 Users and businesses need confirmation that:

   Payments are complete
   Funds are safe

### 3. Enables Real-World Use Cases

 Payments
 Smart contracts
 DeFi systems

---

# 3. Types of Finality

Blockchain systems use two main types of finality:

---

## A. Probabilistic Finality

Used in systems like:

 Bitcoin
 Ethereum

### How it Works

 Transactions become more secure as more blocks are added
 But never 100% guaranteed instantly

### Example

 In Bitcoin:

   1 confirmation → weak
   6 confirmations → considered final

### Key Idea

👉 The probability of reversal becomes extremely low, but not zero.

### Problem

 Chain reorganization (reorg) can still occur

---

## B. Deterministic Finality (Absolute Finality)

Used in systems like:

 Ethereum
 Cosmos
 Polkadot

### How it Works

 Once a block is finalized → it cannot be reversed

### Key Idea

👉 Mathematical guarantee of finality

### Example

 Validators vote on blocks
 Once consensus is reached → block is locked

---

# 4. Finality in Different Consensus Mechanisms

### 1. Proof of Work (PoW)

 Used by Bitcoin
 Finality = probabilistic
 Based on:

   Longest chain rule
   Mining power

---

### 2. Proof of Stake (PoS)

 Used by Ethereum

 Finality = deterministic (via finality gadgets)

Example:

 Ethereum uses:

   Casper FFG (Friendly Finality Gadget)

👉 Blocks are finalized after validator voting rounds

---

### 3. PBFT-Based Systems

 Used in permissioned blockchains

Finality:

 Instant and deterministic

Example systems:

 Hyperledger Fabric

---

# 5. Time to Finality

Different blockchains have different speeds:

 Bitcoin → ~60 minutes (6 confirmations)
 Ethereum → ~12–15 minutes (finalization epochs)
 PBFT systems → seconds

👉 Trade-off:

 Faster finality = less decentralization (often)
 Slower finality = more security (in PoW)

---

# 6. Finality vs Confirmation

| Concept      | Meaning                         |
| ------------ | ------------------------------- |
| Confirmation | Transaction included in a block |
| Finality     | Transaction cannot be reversed  |

👉 Important:

 All finalized transactions are confirmed
 But not all confirmed transactions are finalized

---

# 7. What Can Break Finality?

Even though finality is strong, some risks exist:

### 1. 51% Attack (PoW)

 Majority miner can rewrite history

### 2. Validator Attacks (PoS)

 If enough validators collude

### 3. Network Partitions

 Temporary forks

---

# 8. Real-World Insight

 In financial systems, finality is mandatory
 Example:

   Banks require instant settlement finality

👉 That’s why newer blockchains focus heavily on:

 Faster finality
 Stronger guarantees
