## 24. Proof of Stake (PoS)

Proof of Stake (PoS) is a consensus mechanism used in blockchains to validate transactions and create new blocks without heavy computation like Proof of Work (PoW).

Instead of miners competing with computing power, PoS uses validators who are selected based on how much cryptocurrency they “stake” (lock up).

---

# 1. Why Proof of Stake is Needed

PoS was introduced to solve major problems in PoW:

### Problems with PoW:

 Extremely high energy consumption
 Requires expensive hardware (GPUs/ASICs)
 Leads to centralization (only big players can mine)
 Slower scalability

### PoS Solution:

 No mining → energy efficient
 No need for expensive hardware
 More accessible participation
 Better scalability

---

# 2. Core Idea of PoS

👉 “The more you stake, the higher your chance to validate blocks”

Instead of solving puzzles:

 Validators lock coins as stake
 Network randomly selects a validator
 Selected validator:

   Creates the next block
   Validates transactions
   Earns rewards

---

# 3. How PoS Works (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Staking

 A user locks some cryptocurrency (e.g., ETH)
 This stake acts as security deposit

### Step 2: Validator Selection

 The network selects validators based on:

   Amount staked
   Randomization
   Sometimes staking duration

### Step 3: Block Validation

 Selected validator:

   Verifies transactions
   Creates a block
   Adds it to blockchain

### Step 4: Reward / Penalty

 Honest validator → gets reward
 Malicious validator → loses stake (slashing)

---

# 4. Key Concepts in PoS

### 1. Validators

 Replace miners
 Responsible for block creation

### 2. Staking

 Locking crypto to participate

### 3. Slashing

 Punishment for bad behavior
 Example:

   Double signing
   Invalid transactions

### 4. Finality

 Once confirmed, blocks become irreversible faster than PoW

---

# 5. Example (Simple Understanding)

Imagine:

 3 validators:

   A → 10 coins
   B → 50 coins
   C → 100 coins

👉 C has the highest probability to be selected
👉 But selection is still partly random

---

# 6. Advantages of PoS

### ✔ Energy Efficient

 No heavy computations

### ✔ More Decentralized (in theory)

 Anyone with coins can participate

### ✔ Faster Transactions

 Better scalability

### ✔ Lower Entry Barrier

 No need for expensive hardware

---

# 7. Disadvantages of PoS

### ✖ Wealth Concentration

 Rich validators can dominate

### ✖ Nothing-at-Stake Problem

 Validators might validate multiple chains (solved in modern PoS with slashing)

### ✖ Initial Distribution Problem

 Early holders get more power
