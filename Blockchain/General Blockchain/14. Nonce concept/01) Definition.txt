
In blockchain, a nonce (short for “number used once”) is a value that is used only a single time in a cryptographic process. Its exact role depends on the context, but the core idea is the same: it helps ensure uniqueness and security.

### 🔑 Main uses of a nonce in blockchain

#### 1. In mining (Proof of Work)

 Miners try to find a nonce that, when combined with block data and hashed, produces a valid hash (meeting difficulty rules).
 They repeatedly change the nonce and hash the block until they find a valid result.

👉 Think of it like guessing a number until the output fits certain conditions.

#### 2. In transactions (e.g., Ethereum)

 A nonce is a counter attached to each account.
 It increases by 1 for every transaction sent from that account.

This ensures:

 Transactions are processed in order
 Prevents duplicate or replayed transactions

---

### 🧠 Simple analogy

Imagine sending numbered letters:

 Letter #1, #2, #3…
 If someone tries to resend #2 again, it’s rejected because the system expects #4 next.

That “number” is the nonce.
